# Industry Demo Path Requirements

Date: 2026-05-28
Baseline: V51.1

## Purpose

CyberShield must support multiple serious demo paths because MJC's founder-led go-to-market network spans multiple high-trust buyer ecosystems.  These demos must be deep enough to survive review by knowledgeable industry operators.

## Strategic rule

Do not build generic industry demos.

Each demo path must contain enough sector-specific language, scenarios, evidence types, scoring emphasis, report outputs, and framework overlays to be credible.

## Shared engine, tailored lens

CyberShield must avoid becoming a pile of custom demos.  The architecture should use:

- one control-plane engine
- multiple industry profiles
- multiple role lenses
- multiple scenario packs
- shared scoring model foundation
- shared evidence and report registries

## First-class demo paths

### 1. Defense Industrial Base / Federal Contractor SMB

Audience:

- SMBs supporting DoD or federal work
- federal contractors and subcontractors
- organizations facing CMMC/NIST 800-171 pressure
- companies with CUI, contract, subcontractor, vendor, and evidence-readiness concerns

Avoid overly generic language.  Do not assume every SMB likes being called a “government contractor.”  Use language such as:

- Defense Industrial Base organization
- Federal Contractor SMB
- Small or mid-sized business supporting DoD or federal work
- Federal-adjacent SMB

Primary demo moment:

The executive realizes they may lack enough visibility to prove CUI handling, vendor governance, AI usage, and evidence readiness before contract impact.

Key scenarios:

- CUI uploaded to unapproved AI tool
- subcontractor evidence gap
- vendor governance missing
- policy/evidence mismatch
- privileged access or identity weakness
- executive cannot prove readiness

Primary reports:

- Executive Briefing Summary
- Operational Trust Roadmap
- Proof Pack
- Evidence Register

### 2. Financial Services / Community Banking

Audience:

- community banks
- regional banks
- credit unions
- financial services firms
- financial leadership and board stakeholders

Primary demo moment:

The executive realizes vendor, payment, AI, fraud, and customer-trust risk may be operating beyond current visibility.

Key scenarios:

- AI-assisted vendor payment approval
- payment destination authenticity failure
- fake customer-facing site or ad
- vendor governance review overdue
- AI usage lacks board-level visibility
- operational continuity gap

Primary reports:

- Executive Briefing Summary
- TrustMap / Authenticity Trust Summary
- Operational Trust Roadmap
- Proof Pack

### 3. AI-Enabled SMB / SaaS / Professional Services

Audience:

- AI-enabled SMBs
- SaaS firms
- IT and professional services firms
- organizations adopting AI faster than governance can track

Primary demo moment:

The executive realizes AI adoption has outpaced governance visibility, authority boundaries, evidence readiness, and decision accountability.

Key scenarios:

- shadow AI data upload
- AI-generated customer communication without review
- agentic workflow acts outside authority boundary
- vendor tool adopted without data classification
- unapproved AI system creates reputational or contractual risk

Primary reports:

- Executive Briefing Summary
- AI Governance Readiness Summary
- Operational Trust Roadmap
- Proof Pack

### 4. Healthcare / Federal Health Security

Audience:

- healthcare organizations
- federal health support environments
- HHS/CMS/CDC-adjacent security and governance stakeholders
- organizations handling sensitive health or operational data

Primary demo moment:

The executive realizes patient-data, vendor, AI, evidence, and continuity risk may not be visible enough before compliance, trust, or operational consequence.

Key scenarios:

- sensitive health data uploaded to unapproved AI tool
- third-party healthcare system lacks evidence
- AI-generated patient-facing or administrative output lacks review
- access-control or audit evidence gap
- operational continuity impact from governance fragmentation

Primary reports:

- Executive Briefing Summary
- Evidence Register
- Operational Trust Roadmap
- Proof Pack

## Framework priority

Core frameworks:

1. NIST CSF 2.0
2. NIST AI RMF
3. CMMC / NIST SP 800-171
4. FAIR-style exposure logic as a supporting calculation layer

Industry overlays later:

- FFIEC for financial services
- HIPAA/Security Rule alignment for healthcare
- ISO 27001 for broader control maturity

Compliance should appear as a governance consequence, not the primary platform category.

## Demo quality requirement

Each industry demo must answer:

- What is the risky action?
- What did CyberShield decide?
- Why does it matter in this industry?
- What evidence is missing?
- Who owns the response?
- Which model/framework is being used?
- What report or proof can be exported?
- What advisory assessment should happen next?

## Failure conditions

A demo path fails if:

- it feels generic
- it overclaims live capability
- it uses irrelevant framework language
- it fails to change by role
- it lacks believable scenarios
- it lacks evidence requirements
- it cannot route to a report/export
- a knowledgeable operator would call it shallow
