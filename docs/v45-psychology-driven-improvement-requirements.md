# CyberShield V45 Psychology-Driven Improvement Requirements

Source: colleague feedback based on Robert Greene transcript review

Purpose: Improve CyberShield's executive persuasion, trust perception, urgency, and authority without manipulating users or overclaiming capability.

## Primary principle

CyberShield must not only measure trust.  It must project trust.

The product should feel calm, authoritative, controlled, and evidence-backed.  It should not feel like a generic dashboard, chatbot, compliance checklist, or academic governance concept.

## Design doctrine

CyberShield should occupy the position of authority without becoming manipulative or reckless.

The product should use short executive language, clear decisions, visible consequences, accountable owners, proof-backed confidence, and ethical urgency before damage occurs.

## Executive first-impression requirement

The first 9 seconds must answer:

- What risky action is happening?
- What did CyberShield decide?
- Why does it matter?
- What happens next?

Lead with consequence, not product architecture.

Example opening frame:

```text
An AI workflow is about to send regulated customer data to an unapproved vendor.

Decision: BLOCK

Why:
- Vendor approval missing
- Data classification unclear
- No accountable owner documented
```

## Language requirements

Use short, decisive executive language.

Preferred language:

- Allow
- Constrain
- Escalate
- Block
- Evidence missing
- Owner required
- Decision recorded
- Proof Pack generated
- Not ready to execute
- Ready with constraints
- This action should not execute yet
- Trust is degrading
- Evidence is not sufficient
- Vendor trust is unverified
- Business exposure is active
- Proof Pack is not audit-ready
- Commitment boundary reached
- Human approval required before execution

Avoid weak language:

- may possibly
- could maybe
- might help
- intended to support
- potentially useful

## Required V45 modules

### 1. Pressure Points module

Add a Pressure Points module to the executive dashboard.

Purpose: create ethical urgency without hype.

Pressure Points should show:

- expired evidence
- missing owner
- unapproved vendor
- unresolved override
- missing proof pack
- policy conflict
- escalation overdue
- AI usage expanding faster than controls

Example:

```text
Pressure Points
1. Vendor review expired 47 days ago
2. Data classification missing for selected workflow
3. CISO approval required before execution
4. Two overrides remain open
5. Proof Pack is not board-ready
```

### 2. Executive Commitment Record

CyberShield should convert decisions into accountable commitments.

Required fields:

- Decision owner
- Commitment
- Due date
- Status
- Proof Pack impact
- Escalation path

Example:

```text
Decision owner: CISO
Commitment: Refresh vendor AI review and assign data owner before allowing customer data upload
Due date: June 7, 2026
Status: Open
Proof Pack impact: Board-ready only after commitment is closed
```

### 3. TrustMap power network

TrustMap should feel like a power network, not an isolated diagram.

TrustMap should show CyberShield as the trust coordination layer across:

- CEO / President
- CFO
- CIO / CTO
- CISO / vCISO
- Legal
- Board / Advisor
- Auditor
- Vendor
- AI system / agent
- Data owner
- Customer-impacting workflow

### 4. Evidence-backed confidence

Every CyberShield decision must show:

- decision outcome
- decision rationale
- evidence used
- evidence missing
- owner required
- consequence avoided
- proof generated

Example:

```text
Decision: Block

Because:
- Vendor approval is missing
- Data classification is unclear
- Authority owner is not documented

Proof generated:
- Decision record
- Evidence gap log
- Owner assignment
- Proof Pack
```

### 5. Before Consequence moment

Add a visual moment showing CyberShield caught the action before damage occurred.

Example:

```text
Without CyberShield:
Action executes first

With CyberShield:
Action stopped before data movement

Outcome:
Exposure avoided
Decision recorded
Owner assigned
Proof generated
```

### 6. Result-backed authority

For every trust score, show:

- top 3 score reducers
- top 3 score improvers
- evidence supporting current score
- missing evidence preventing higher confidence

Example:

```text
Trust Score: 61
Score Confidence: Medium

Score reducers:
- Vendor approval missing
- Data classification unclear
- Owner approval undocumented

Score improvers:
- Attach vendor review: +9
- Confirm data classification: +7
- Assign CISO approval: +6
```

### 7. Deadline pressure

CyberShield should allow deadline assignment for unresolved trust gaps.

Example:

```text
Evidence gap: Vendor AI review missing
Owner: CISO
Deadline: 7 days
If unresolved: Escalate to CEO / Board-ready risk item
```

### 8. Power of Proof design

The Proof Pack should feel like the product's authority artifact.

Proof Pack framing:

> The executive record that shows what was reviewed, what was decided, why it was decided, what evidence existed, what evidence was missing, who owned the action, and what consequence was avoided.

## Claim boundaries

Allowed claim:

> CyberShield models executive trust decisions and generates proof-ready decision records.

Do not claim unless integrations exist:

- CyberShield enforces production AI actions
- CyberShield blocks live systems
- CyberShield integrates with SIEM, EDR, IAM, Microsoft 365, GRC, or CRM

## V45 success test

A first-time executive should feel within 60 seconds:

- CyberShield sees the dangerous action
- CyberShield knows what evidence matters
- CyberShield knows who should own the decision
- CyberShield can explain why the action should be allowed, constrained, escalated, or blocked
- CyberShield creates proof leadership can use
- CyberShield creates urgency without hype

## Future builder instruction

Treat this document as a product psychology and executive persuasion layer.  It should improve clarity, authority, and urgency.  It must not be used to manipulate users, exaggerate capability, hide uncertainty, or overclaim production enforcement.

Recommended implementation window:

- After V29 integration-readiness decisions are documented
- Before any external executive demo or partner review

Recommended module mapping:

- Briefing: first 9 seconds, consequence-first decision frame, Pressure Points
- Runtime: evidence-backed confidence, result-backed authority, Before Consequence moment
- TrustMap: power network across executive and operational roles
- Reports: Power of Proof, Proof Pack authority artifact
- Memory: Executive Commitment Record and deadline pressure
- Pilot: V45 success test and ethical urgency framing
