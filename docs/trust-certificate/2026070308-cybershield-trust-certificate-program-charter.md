## 1. Program purpose

The CyberShield Trust Certificate Program will create, operate, commercialize, and continuously improve a decision-level AI assurance capability.

The program will evaluate consequential decisions supported or generated by artificial intelligence and issue a bounded, evidence-backed CyberShield AI Trust Decision Certificate when the applicable assurance requirements have been satisfied.

The program will begin as a commercially viable certificate and assurance service.  It may mature into an independently governed certification scheme supported by qualified assessors, recurring reassessment, external recognition, and enterprise procurement and insurance use cases.

## 2. Mission

Enable organizations to demonstrate that a specific AI-supported decision has been evaluated against transparent requirements for evidence quality, confidence, Risk-if-Wrong, controls, human oversight, intended use, and residual risk.

The program converts AI assurance from an informal opinion into a verifiable, repeatable, and commercially valuable decision record.

## 3. Vision

The CyberShield AI Trust Decision Certificate becomes a recognized standard of record for determining whether an organization can responsibly rely upon a consequential AI-supported decision.

CyberShield will provide the trust infrastructure through which organizations can:

- Evaluate AI-supported decisions.
- Document why a decision should or should not be trusted.
- Establish the conditions under which the decision may be used.
- Demonstrate responsible review to customers, boards, regulators, insurers, auditors, and procurement officials.
- Reassess, suspend, revoke, or supersede assurance when material conditions change.

## 4. Strategic positioning

The current product is a **Trust Certificate**, not a claim that CyberShield is an accredited certification body.

### Initial position

CyberShield provides an evidence-backed decision assurance assessment and issues a certificate describing:

- What decision was evaluated.
- What evidence was considered.
- What risks were identified.
- What confidence can reasonably be assigned.
- What controls or conditions are required.
- What limitations and residual risks remain.

### Future position

At higher maturity levels, the program may become a formal Trust Certification scheme involving:

- A published certification standard.
- Independent assessors.
- Assessor qualification requirements.
- Certification governance separated from readiness consulting.
- Appeals and disputes.
- Surveillance and reassessment.
- Certificate suspension and revocation.
- External accreditation or recognition.

- Assessment of human oversight requirements.

- Generation of the AI Trust Decision Record.

- Certificate issuance, verification, expiration, suspension, revocation, and supersession.
- Certificate registry and verification services.
- Quality assurance and methodology validation.
- Internal pilots and external customer pilots.

- Training and qualification of reviewers and future assessors.
- Partnerships with technology providers, auditors, insurers, law firms, and governance organizations.
- Continuous program measurement and improvement.

### 5.2 Initially out of scope

The first commercial release will not claim to provide:

- Universal certification that an AI model or agent is trustworthy for every use.
- Certification of an entire enterprise AI management system.
- Regulatory approval.
- Legal advice or legal certification.
- A guarantee that an AI-supported decision is correct.
- A guarantee that no harm, loss, bias, hallucination, or security incident will occur.
- Formal accreditation under an external certification-body standard.
- Insurance coverage or underwriting.
- Fully autonomous certificate issuance without accountable human review.

## 6. Unit of assurance

The primary unit of assurance is the **AI-supported decision**, not the entire model, agent, application, vendor, or organization.

Each certificate must be bound to:

- A specific decision or recommendation.
- A defined intended use.
- A defined operating context.
- An identified AI system or systems.
- Applicable model and system versions.
- A defined evidence set.
- A defined assessment date.
- A defined methodology version.
- Defined limitations and prohibited uses.

A certificate issued for one decision may not be represented as assurance for unrelated decisions or uses.

## 7. Certificate outcomes

### Assured

The evidence, controls, confidence, oversight, and residual risk satisfy the applicable assurance threshold.

### Assured with Conditions

The decision may proceed only when listed controls, restrictions, validations, or human approvals are implemented.

### Insufficient Assurance

The available evidence is insufficient to support the decision.  This outcome does not automatically mean the decision is incorrect or unsafe.

### Not Assured

Material contradictions, unacceptable risk, control failures, unreliable evidence, or prohibited conditions prevent reliance upon the decision.

## 10. Maturity model

### Level 0: Concept

Mission, scope, governance, certificate concept, and backlog are defined.  No controlled operating process exists.

**Exit criteria:** Charter approved, program owner assigned, terminology approved, and initial backlog established.

### Level 1: Defined Certificate

The standard, certificate template, controlled workflow, mandatory human review, and manual certificate tracking exist.

**Exit criteria:** End-to-end workflow operates, five internal decisions are assessed, certificate language receives legal review, and a quality checklist is operational.

### Level 2: Repeatable Assurance Service

Standard intake, evidence requirements, reviewer procedures, verification records, controlled paid pilots, and measurable time and cost exist.

**Exit criteria:** Ten completed assessments, three external pilots, one paid certificate, repeatable delivery, and lifecycle procedures.

### Level 3: Commercial Trust Certificate Program

Published service packages, enterprise contracts, repeat customers, registry, reviewer qualification, sales support, and recurring revenue exist.

**Exit criteria:** Three paying organizations, one repeat customer or subscription, twenty-five paid certificates, and acceptable quality and margin performance.

### Level 4: Independently Assured Certification Scheme

Independent assessors, separated scheme governance, appeals, surveillance, assessor monitoring, and controlled certification-mark use exist.

**Exit criteria:** Independent certifications completed, reviewer consistency evaluated, governance independently reviewed, and external stakeholders accept the certificate as assurance evidence.

### Level 5: Recognized Trust Infrastructure

Machine-verifiable certificates, continuous monitoring, ecosystem integrations, diversified revenue, and external reliance exist.

**Exit criteria:** Sustained enterprise adoption, material recurring revenue, and demonstrated use in procurement, audit, governance, or underwriting.

## 13. Definition of commercial viability

The initial program is commercially viable when:

- An external organization pays for a CyberShield AI Trust Decision Certificate.
- The certificate is delivered through a repeatable process.
- The customer recognizes it as useful assurance evidence.
- The delivery price exceeds the complete assessment, review, support, and risk cost.
- The customer requests another assessment, renewal, subscription, or related service.

The strongest validation is repeat purchasing or recurring enterprise use, not the first sale.

## 15. Program success definition

The program is successful when organizations voluntarily pay CyberShield to evaluate and certify the assurance of consequential AI-supported decisions and rely upon the resulting certificates in meaningful governance, procurement, risk, audit, insurance, or executive processes.

Long-term success is demonstrated when:

- Demand is driven by customers, boards, procurement teams, insurers, or regulators rather than solely by CyberShield marketing.
- Enterprises require or prefer the certificate for defined classes of AI-supported decisions.
- The program produces sustainable recurring revenue.
- External assessors and partners participate.
- CyberShield certificates become recognized trust evidence.
- The program measurably improves the transparency and defensibility of AI-supported decisions.
