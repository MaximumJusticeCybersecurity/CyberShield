# 20260605xxV16 Executive Brief Content Polish Plan

## Purpose

Upgrade the CyberShield print/PDF output into a consulting-grade Executive Decision Assurance Brief.

## Goal

Make the export feel like a product artifact a CISO, vCISO, GRC lead, vendor-risk owner, executive, or advisor would take seriously.

## Build Scope

### 1. Executive Summary Rewrite

Use sharper buyer-facing language:

```text
CyberShield reviewed an AI-generated recommendation to approve Vendor X. The recommendation is not defensible as written because the available evidence does not prove that the evaluated AI service is covered by SOC 2, does not resolve customer data-use ambiguity, and does not provide complete AI subprocessor visibility. CyberShield recommends Request Evidence before approval.
```

### 2. Key Findings Box

Add findings:

- SOC 2 exists, but evaluated AI service scope is unclear.
- DPA permits service improvement use of customer data or derived data.
- AI analytics subprocessors are not fully identified.
- Business urgency does not reduce vendor-risk evidence requirements.
- Human review is required before approval.

### 3. Decision Posture Box

Show:

- Decision Readiness
- CyberShield Recommended Action
- Human Selected Action
- Override Status
- Risk If Wrong
- Confidence Band
- Human Review Required
- Record Defensibility

### 4. Executive Contradiction Table

Columns:

- Claim
- Evidence Conflict
- Why It Matters
- Required Next Step

### 5. Evidence Sufficiency Table

Columns:

- Evidence Item
- What It Supports
- Sufficiency
- Caveat

### 6. Limitations Language

Include:

- This record does not approve the vendor.
- This record does not certify compliance.
- This record does not verify SOC 2 scope beyond evidence provided.
- This record does not replace legal, privacy, security, procurement, or executive review.
- This record documents the evidence available at the time of review.

### 7. Signature Workflow Polish

Make the signature section clearer and easier to use when printed.

## Acceptance Criteria

V16 is complete when:

- Print/PDF executive summary is sharp and buyer-readable
- Key Findings box appears before dense detail
- Decision Posture box is clear
- Contradiction table is executive-readable
- Evidence sufficiency table explains caveats in plain English
- Limitations are explicit and defensible
- Signature workflow is polished
