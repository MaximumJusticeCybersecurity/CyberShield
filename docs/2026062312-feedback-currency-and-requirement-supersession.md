# CyberShield Feedback Currency and Requirement Supersession

Version timestamp: 2026062312  
Status: Proposed repository operating requirement  
Owner: Dr. Max Justice  
Baseline reviewed: CyberShield `main` at `cecabf277a381d12671a7ebe3e15efc31420ff35`

## Purpose

Prevent delayed founder, advisor, buyer, builder, or agent feedback from silently restoring outdated product scope or conflicting with newer approved direction.

## Required decision fields

Every material feedback item shall include:

```text
Feedback ID:
Source:
Source role:
Originally expressed:
Received or recovered:
Build / route / artifact reviewed:
Baseline commit:
Current-state comparison:
Decision: Accept | Accept with constraints | Defer | Reject | Superseded | Needs owner clarification
Reason:
Requirements affected:
Prior requirement superseded:
Owner approval status:
Target mission:
```

## Currency rules

1. Feedback arrival date does not equal decision date.
2. Newer feedback does not automatically win if it was formed against an older build.
3. Current explicit owner direction wins when recorded against the current state.
4. Security, human authority, evidence, and claims boundaries cannot be weakened by sales feedback.
5. A durable buyer problem may remain valid even when the proposed feature or route is stale.
6. Preserve the useful insight while rejecting obsolete implementation detail.
7. Stop implementation when two active requirements conflict and no supersession record resolves them.

## Current effective direction

Effective 2026062312:

   - CyberShield remains AI Decision Assurance.
   - The AI Trust Decision Record remains the product.
   - Vendor-risk remains the first proof point.
   - The primary customer action becomes `Challenge One AI Recommendation`.
   - The demonstration shall show value before requesting unnecessary information.
   - Public pages shall not expose internal route, fallback, build, capture, or QA language.
   - A bounded 3-to-5 recommendation advisory pilot is the commercial next step.
   - Exact price and delivery timing remain proposed and owner-gated.

## Explicit supersession

The following earlier decisions are no longer controlling for the primary customer path:

| Earlier direction | Current disposition | Current direction |
|---|---|---|
| TrustMap is the flagship CyberShield conversion surface | Superseded for primary conversion | AI Decision Assurance and the AI Trust Decision Record |
| Multiple industry demos are an immediate priority | Deferred | Make vendor-risk conversion and pilot movement work first |
| First name, company, and vendor are collected at the start | Superseded | Show recommendation and evidence problem before data capture |
| `Try a Vendor-Risk Sample` is the primary CTA | Superseded | `Challenge One AI Recommendation` |
| Generic `Request a Demo` is the principal sales step | Superseded where a concrete action is available | Review one real recommendation, then pilot |
| Runtime intervention proof is the immediate commercial proof | Deferred / separate future scope | Recommendation review and defensible record first |

## Implementation rule

A builder shall use the current dated content requirements and record which older sections are superseded.  Do not rewrite preserved historical documents merely to make history appear consistent.

## Review trigger

Re-run feedback reconciliation when:

   - New owner feedback changes category, CTA, buyer, pilot, price, or product boundary.
   - Advisor feedback arrives more than seven days after the reviewed build.
   - A route or document still uses TrustMap-first, runtime-first, or broad-platform language.
   - A public page conflicts with `governance-summary.json`.
   - A builder proposes to use an older requirements document as authority.

## Core rule

The repository records both what was believed and what now controls.  History is preserved; current authority is explicit.
