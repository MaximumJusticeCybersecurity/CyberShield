# 20260605xxV10 ATDR Market Validation Note

## Purpose

Validate whether CyberShield AI Decision Assurance creates real buyer pull.

## Thesis

CyberShield creates decision evidence before AI-generated advice becomes enterprise action.

## What To Validate

1. Is the pain real?
2. Is the buyer identifiable?
3. Is the pilot offer easy to understand?
4. Is the AI Trust Decision Record the right artifact?
5. Does the phrase decision evidence land?

## Validation Waves

### Wave 1: Trusted Advisors

Goal:

- Stress-test the story
- Identify strongest buyer path
- Surface objections
- Improve demo language

### Wave 2: Buyer-Adjacent Reviewers

Goal:

- Test workflow relevance
- Validate evidence gaps
- Validate review ownership
- Validate pilot scope

### Wave 3: Pilot Candidates

Goal:

- Identify real recommendations to review
- Confirm decision owner
- Scope a pilot conversation

## Reviewer Segments

- CISO / vCISO
- GRC / Compliance
- Vendor Risk / Procurement
- Executive / Investor
- AI Governance

## Must-Ask Questions

1. Where would this prevent a bad AI-influenced decision?
2. Who owns that pain today?
3. Who has budget to solve it?
4. What existing process or tool would this be compared against?
5. Would a 3 to 5 recommendation pilot make sense?
6. What would make you dismiss this?

## Strong Signal

- Reviewer names a real use case
- Reviewer names a buyer title
- Reviewer names a budget owner
- Reviewer asks about a pilot
- Reviewer requests the executive brief
- Reviewer introduces another stakeholder

## Weak Signal

- Reviewer says it is interesting but names no use case
- Reviewer cannot identify buyer
- Reviewer treats it as a dashboard
- Reviewer does not see urgency

## After 10 Reviews

Summarize:

- Top three use cases
- Strongest buyer title
- Most common objection
- Best repeated language
- Recommended pilot scope
- Product changes needed before wider demos

## Rule

Do not optimize for praise.

Optimize for evidence of urgent buyer pain.
