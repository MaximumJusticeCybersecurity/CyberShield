# AIDA Decision Assurance Lexicon

Status: Initial controlled baseline  
Owner: Dr. Max Justice

## Purpose

Provide one authoritative working definition for the primary AIDA terms.  Existing protected CyberShield schema definitions remain controlling where a conflict exists until the conflict is formally reconciled.

## Terms

### AI-generated recommendation

A proposed conclusion, action, prioritization, interpretation, or decision produced or materially shaped by an AI system.

### Claim

A statement presented as true or sufficiently reliable to support a recommendation.

### Evidence

Information that materially supports or challenges a claim and can be inspected, attributed, and evaluated for relevance, authority, freshness, independence, and reliability.

### Missing evidence

Information required to justify a claim or action that is unavailable, absent, inaccessible, stale, or not supplied.

### Contradiction

Evidence or a materially credible claim that conflicts with another claim, assumption, inference, or recommendation.

### Assumption

A proposition accepted for analysis without sufficient direct evidence.

### Inference

A conclusion derived from evidence and reasoning rather than directly observed as a fact.

### Recommendation

A proposed course of action or decision based on claims, evidence, assumptions, inferences, constraints, and objectives.

### Trust

A continuously evaluated judgment that a recommendation is sufficiently supported and bounded for a defined purpose, context, consequence, and period.  Trust is not a permanent attribute of a model.

### Confidence

The justified degree of belief that the recommendation is adequately supported for the stated use.  Confidence must be explained and shall not be treated as evidence.

### Risk if wrong

The credible consequence of acting on an incorrect, incomplete, manipulated, stale, or misapplied recommendation.

### Decision assurance

The disciplined evaluation of whether an AI-generated recommendation is sufficiently supported, explainable, bounded, and safe enough to influence a human or autonomous decision.

### Decision admissibility

The determination that a recommendation has met the minimum evidence, consequence, confidence, authority, and review requirements for the proposed use.  Admissibility does not require acceptance or execution.

### Meaningful human authority

A human review condition in which the accountable person can understand, challenge, modify, reject, defer, escalate, or accept the recommendation and record the rationale.

### Ceremonial approval

A nominal human gate where the reviewer lacks sufficient information, time, authority, alternatives, or practical ability to disagree.

### AI Trust Decision Record

The public CyberShield artifact that preserves the recommendation, claims, evidence, missing evidence, assumptions, contradictions, risk if wrong, confidence explanation, required review, system recommendation, and final accountable human decision.

### Provenance

The traceable history of an artifact, source, transformation, model, prompt, tool, or decision record.  Provenance supports evaluation but does not prove truth.

### Source independence

The degree to which multiple sources do not merely repeat the same underlying assertion, vendor, dataset, model output, or compromised origin.

### Decision owner

The accountable human with authority and responsibility for the final consequential decision.

### No-action outcome

A legitimate conclusion that no additional intervention, purchase, control, or execution is presently justified.

### Challenge-tested

A status showing that the recommendation was deliberately examined through skeptical, adversarial, contradictory, or alternative reasoning.

### Model-agnostic

Able to operate without depending on one model provider, model family, agent framework, or proprietary AI platform.

### Vendor-neutral

Able to evaluate recommendations independently of the commercial interests of the model, platform, consultant, or tool provider.
