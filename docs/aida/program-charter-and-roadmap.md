# AIDA Program Charter and Roadmap

Owner and executive sponsor: Dr. Max Justice  
Status: Approved  
Priority: High

## Mission

Establish AI Decision Assurance as the discipline that enables organizations to determine whether AI-generated recommendations are trustworthy enough to influence human or autonomous decisions.

## Strategic uses

AIDA is the foundation for:

- CyberShield;
- MJC advisory and vCISO services;
- AI Decision Assurance Assessments;
- executive workshops;
- customer discovery;
- training and certification;
- white papers and research;
- future patents and publications; and
- future platform integrations.

## Seven phases

### Phase 1: Decision Assurance Playbook

Document the operational methodology for recommendation intake, claim extraction, evidence review, missing evidence, assumptions, contradiction handling, risk if wrong, confidence, human authority, and decision recording.

### Phase 2: AI Trust Decision Record Standard

Define the canonical human-readable record and ensure compatibility with the protected CyberShield schema and contract.

### Phase 3: AI Decision Assurance Assessment

Create a service-led consulting offer that evaluates a customer's current AI decision workflow, controls, evidence, ownership, and adoption readiness.

### Phase 4: CyberShield Product Integration

Trace every material CyberShield feature to an AIDA principle, customer pain point, buyer, measurable outcome, and AI Trust Decision Record element.

### Phase 5: Customer Discovery

Interview CISOs, CIOs, CFOs, risk leaders, compliance leaders, auditors, federal leaders, healthcare leaders, financial-services leaders, and assurance providers.  Observe the actual day-in-the-life workflow rather than asking only whether participants like the concept.

### Phase 6: AI Decision Assurance Maturity Model

Develop a maturity model that may include:

1. Blind Trust
2. Human Validation
3. Evidence-Based Trust
4. Runtime Decision Assurance
5. Continuous Decision Governance

The labels and thresholds remain hypotheses until validated.

### Phase 7: Thought Leadership

Develop an executive brief, white paper, conference presentation, webinar, workshop, standards proposal, and eventually a book or certification program.

## Success criteria

AIDA is successful when:

- customer interviews validate a recurring decision-assurance workflow;
- buyers can identify who owns the problem and which budget pays;
- CyberShield produces a defensible record in under five minutes for the pilot case;
- the record improves decision quality, review speed, consistency, or audit defensibility;
- every current feature is traceable to the methodology; and
- the system remains portable across current and future AI platforms.
