# 20260605xxV8 ATDR External Review Guide

## Purpose

Use this guide when showing the CyberShield AI Decision Assurance package to advisors, buyers, partners, investors, or trusted reviewers.

The goal is not compliments.

The goal is evidence about whether the product story, buyer pain, pilot offer, and workflow are strong enough to move forward.

## Start Route

```text
/package.html
```

## Review Sequence

1. Why CyberShield Wins
2. Buyer Paths
3. Pilot Offer
4. ATDR Workbench
5. Executive Brief
6. Evidence Guide
7. Human Review Guide
8. Readiness Verifier

## Opening Frame

Say:

```text
CyberShield turns AI-generated security, compliance, and vendor-risk recommendations into defensible AI Trust Decision Records.
```

Then say:

```text
The question is not whether the AI sounds right.  The question is whether the organization can defend acting on the recommendation based on evidence available at the time.
```

## Questions For Reviewers

### Problem Fit

- Where do you already see teams acting on AI-generated recommendations?
- Which AI-generated recommendations would create the most damage if wrong?
- Is this problem visible to leadership yet, or still hidden inside teams?

### Buyer Fit

- Who would feel this pain first?
- Who would own the budget?
- Who would block the purchase?
- Which title understands the problem fastest?

### Product Fit

- Does the AI Trust Decision Record feel like the right artifact?
- Are claims, evidence, gaps, risk, confidence, and human review the right workflow?
- What part feels unnecessary?
- What part is missing?

### Offer Fit

- Would a 3 to 5 recommendation pilot be a credible first engagement?
- What would make the pilot easier to buy?
- What deliverable would make the pilot more valuable?

### Competitive Fit

- What existing tool would this be compared against?
- Is CyberShield different enough from governance dashboards and GRC tools?
- Does “decision evidence” land as a category wedge?

## Signals To Capture

Strong signal:

- Reviewer names a real AI-influenced decision risk.
- Reviewer identifies a specific buyer.
- Reviewer asks how soon it can be piloted.
- Reviewer wants the executive brief or pilot offer.

Weak signal:

- Reviewer only says it is interesting.
- Reviewer cannot identify a use case.
- Reviewer treats it as a dashboard feature.
- Reviewer thinks it is too broad or unclear.

## Review Outcome Categories

Use one of these outcomes:

1. Strong buyer pull
2. Useful but unclear buyer
3. Good consulting offer, not product yet
4. Product story unclear
5. Not urgent enough
6. Needs narrower wedge

## Follow-Up Action

After each review, capture:

- Reviewer type
- Buyer path they cared about
- Best use case they named
- Strongest objection
- Whether they would accept a follow-up pilot conversation
- Any specific language they repeated back

## Do Not Ask

Do not ask:

```text
Do you like it?
```

Ask:

```text
Where would this prevent a bad AI-influenced decision?
```
