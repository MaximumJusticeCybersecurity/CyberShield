# 20260605xxV9 ATDR Pilot Outreach Package

## Purpose

Provide practical outreach language for introducing CyberShield AI Decision Assurance to advisors, buyers, partners, and trusted contacts.

Goal:

```text
Start a real conversation about AI-generated recommendations that may become business action without a defensible record.
```

## Core Message

```text
CyberShield turns AI-generated security, compliance, and vendor-risk recommendations into AI Trust Decision Records.
```

## Short LinkedIn Message

```text
I’m testing a focused CyberShield workflow: AI-generated recommendation in, AI Trust Decision Record out.

The problem I’m exploring is simple: teams are starting to act on AI-generated security, compliance, and vendor-risk recommendations before anyone can show what claims were made, what evidence supports them, what evidence is missing, what happens if the AI is wrong, and who reviewed the decision.

I’d value your reaction because you see how these decisions actually move inside organizations.  The main question is not “do you like the tool?”  It is: where would this prevent a bad AI-influenced decision?
```

## Advisor Message

```text
I’m looking for sharp feedback on a CyberShield prototype.

The workflow turns AI-generated security, compliance, and vendor-risk recommendations into AI Trust Decision Records.  It extracts claims, maps evidence, identifies gaps, classifies Risk If Wrong, assigns confidence, requires human review where needed, and creates an exportable record.

What I’m trying to validate is whether “decision evidence” is the right wedge.  Most AI governance conversations stay at the policy or dashboard level.  CyberShield focuses on whether a specific AI recommendation is defensible enough to act on.

Would you be willing to review the flow and tell me where you think the strongest buyer pain is?
```

## Potential Buyer Message

```text
I’m building a focused CyberShield workflow for teams that are starting to rely on AI-generated security, compliance, or vendor-risk recommendations.

The workflow asks: before anyone acts, can the organization defend the recommendation based on evidence available at the time?

The output is an AI Trust Decision Record showing claims, evidence, missing support, Risk If Wrong, confidence, human review, and defensible next action.

I’d like to show you the prototype and pressure-test one question: where are AI-generated recommendations already influencing decisions in your environment without a clear review record?
```

## Partner Message

```text
I’m packaging CyberShield AI Decision Assurance as a narrow workflow that could fit alongside vCISO, GRC, vendor-risk, and AI governance advisory work.

It turns AI-generated recommendations into AI Trust Decision Records so a client can see the claims, evidence, missing support, Risk If Wrong, required review, and defensible action.

I’m looking for partners who see clients using AI advice too quickly and need a way to turn that into a reviewable record.

Would you be open to looking at the prototype and discussing whether this could support your client work?
```

## Discovery Call Opening

```text
The demo is short.  I’m not looking for compliments.  I’m looking for whether this prevents a real bad decision.

The question I want to keep coming back to is: where are people already acting on AI-generated recommendations without a defensible record?
```

## Discovery Questions

1. Where is AI already influencing security, compliance, vendor-risk, or procurement decisions?
2. Which AI-generated recommendation would create the most damage if wrong?
3. Who would get blamed if a team acted on bad AI advice?
4. Who would own the review process today?
5. What evidence would be required before acting?
6. What evidence is usually missing?
7. Who would approve a pilot if the pain is real?
8. What would make the pilot too hard to buy?
9. What existing tool or process would CyberShield be compared against?
10. Would a 3 to 5 recommendation sprint be useful?

## Follow-Up Message After Demo

```text
Thank you for reviewing CyberShield AI Decision Assurance.

The most useful thread I heard was [insert pain / use case].  That is exactly the kind of AI-influenced decision risk the AI Trust Decision Record is designed to expose before action.

The next practical step would be to identify 3 to 5 AI-generated recommendations your team may rely on and run them through the workflow as a focused AI Recommendation Assurance Sprint.

The deliverables would be AI Trust Decision Records, an evidence-gap summary, Risk If Wrong classification, human review triggers, and an executive findings brief.
```

## Pilot Close

```text
Can we identify three AI-generated recommendations your organization may act on in the next 30 days and turn them into defensible decision records?
```

## Do Not Say

Do not say:

- CyberShield proves compliance
- CyberShield approves vendors
- CyberShield gives legal advice
- CyberShield replaces the CISO
- CyberShield replaces GRC
- CyberShield is a full AI governance platform today

## Say Instead

```text
CyberShield creates decision evidence before AI-generated advice becomes enterprise action.
```
