## Default simulation

Default scenario:

Shadow AI Vendor Data Upload

Scenario description:

An employee or AI-enabled workflow attempts to upload customer, contract, or regulated business data into an unapproved AI vendor tool.

Without CyberShield:

- The upload executes first
- Data lineage is unclear
- Vendor risk is unknown
- Ownership is disputed
- Audit trail is incomplete
- Business exposure is estimated after damage

With CyberShield:

- Action is checked before execution
- Trust score and score confidence are calculated
- Evidence gaps are surfaced
- Owner is assigned or escalated
- Decision is blocked or escalated
- Proof Pack is generated

## Boundary

CyberShield V44 is still a static advisory prototype.  It does not yet connect to live SIEM, EDR, IAM, Microsoft 365, GRC, CRM, cloud telemetry, or production agent enforcement systems.

It models executive trust decisions, manual evidence intake, directional exposure, role-tailored proof packs, replayable decision records, and future runtime governance architecture.

## Known limitations

- Still static and local/export oriented
- No authentication
- No live backend
- No live CRM append
- No production runtime enforcement
- Business exposure remains directional and should not be represented as a guaranteed financial calculation
- Trust factor scoring is prototype logic and needs validation
- TrustMap icons are inline SVG-like but still need visual polish and brand consistency
- Full browser QA still required
