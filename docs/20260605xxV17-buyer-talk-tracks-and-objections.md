# 20260605xxV17 Buyer Talk Tracks and Objections

## Core Opening Question

If your organization acted on an AI recommendation and that decision was challenged later, what evidence would you show?

## Demo Promise

CyberShield turns AI-assisted recommendations into defensible Trust Decision Records.

## Short Explanation

CyberShield does not ask whether an AI answer sounds right. It asks whether the organization can defend acting on that recommendation based on evidence available at the time.

## Universal Talk Track

CyberShield sits between AI-generated advice and enterprise action.

It extracts claims, maps evidence, shows gaps, identifies contradictions, classifies Risk If Wrong, assigns confidence based on evidence, requires human review where needed, and exports a Trust Decision Record.

The workbench is not the product. The Trust Decision Record is the product.

## Vendor-Risk Talk Track

The vendor-risk demo shows a common failure pattern.

The AI recommendation says Vendor X should be approved because they have SOC 2, encrypt customer data, and appear low risk.

CyberShield shows that SOC 2 exists, but the evaluated AI service is not clearly in scope. It also shows customer data-use ambiguity, incomplete AI subprocessor visibility, and weak incident notification terms.

The conclusion is not that the vendor is bad. The conclusion is that the approval recommendation is not defensible as written.

## CISO and vCISO Talk Track

For CISO and vCISO audiences, emphasize risk acceptance and accountability.

CyberShield helps prevent unsupported AI-generated security recommendations from becoming executive certainty. It shows what evidence is required before accepting risk, changing controls, approving vendor access, or relying on AI-generated security advice.

## GRC and Compliance Talk Track

For GRC and compliance audiences, emphasize evidence sufficiency.

CyberShield separates framework relevance from compliance proof. A policy, SOC 2 summary, or AI-generated framework mapping is not enough unless the supporting evidence proves the specific claim.

Required language remains:

Relevant to framework/control. Not verified as compliant.

## Executive Talk Track

For executive audiences, emphasize hindsight and accountability.

The question is not whether the AI was impressive. The question is whether the organization can show why it acted on the recommendation if the decision is reviewed later.

## Objection: We already review AI outputs.

Response:

Good. CyberShield does not assume review is absent. It documents whether the review is consistent, evidence-based, and defensible later.

## Objection: This sounds like AI judging AI.

Response:

CyberShield is not another AI blessing the answer. It extracts claims, maps evidence, exposes gaps, and requires accountable human review.

## Objection: We already have GRC tools.

Response:

CyberShield creates the decision evidence that can feed GRC. It is not replacing GRC in this prototype.

## Objection: SOC 2 should be enough.

Response:

SOC 2 is evidence, not automatic approval. Scope, date, service coverage, data use, subprocessors, and contract terms still matter.

## Objection: This looks like a consulting service, not a product.

Response:

That is acceptable for the first commercial motion. The fixed-scope sprint creates real Trust Decision Records and shows where the product delivers repeatable value.

## Pilot Close

The pilot is simple: 3 to 5 AI-assisted recommendations, Trust Decision Records, and an Executive Decision Assurance Brief showing what can be defended, what is missing, and who needs to review it.
