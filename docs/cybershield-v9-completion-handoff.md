## Current architecture

CyberShield remains a static HTML/CSS/JavaScript prototype compatible with GitHub Pages and embeddable into Squarespace. No React, Vite, backend service, or complex build pipeline has been introduced.

## Important constraints

The current product is a controlled prototype. It does not claim live enforcement, live telemetry ingestion, SIEM integration, EDR/XDR integration, identity provider integration, graph database persistence, or production audit storage.

## Human-in-the-loop doctrine

CyberShield does not replace leadership judgment. It structures leadership judgment by personalizing recommendations to the named human in the loop, their role, decision authority, evidence confidence, and business context.

## Validation checklist

After deployment, test the following:

1. Clear browser state or reset assessment
2. Start Executive Trust Assessment
3. Confirm guided onboarding appears in steps
4. Submit a profile with multiple roles
5. Confirm dashboard role switching appears
6. Confirm Escalation, Evidence, and Owner are explained in the first briefing
7. Open Admissibility
8. Select several AI or automated actions
9. Save one decision to Memory
10. Open the Memory tab and confirm the decision appears
11. Open Reports
12. Open Board Decision Brief
13. Open Executive Proof Pack
14. Open Roadmap
15. Download Roadmap Text
16. Open TrustMap and confirm legend and maturity path appear
17. Open Settings and confirm build continuity and validation checklist appear
18. Test desktop, tablet, and mobile widths

## Recommended next product phase

The next meaningful product phase should move beyond static simulation into one of these directions:

1. Google Apps Script intake persistence into Google Sheets
2. Lightweight backend storage for assessments and saved decision records
3. Real report generation as downloadable HTML/PDF artifacts
4. More advanced TrustMap interactions driven by selected actions and saved evidence
5. A Squarespace embed package with lead capture handoff to MJC
6. A CRM-linked follow-up path for MJC advisory services

## Guardrails for the next builder

1. Do not expose raw chain-of-thought
2. Use Decision Rationale Trace, Evidence Path, and Executive Decision Record language
3. Do not make CyberShield sound like a chatbot or MSP portal
4. Do not introduce a complex build stack without a strong reason
5. Do not remove Executive Advisor Layer language
6. Do not remove individualization by named human and role
7. Do not merge CyberShield platform code into the MJC website codebase
8. Do not claim production enforcement until actual integrations exist
9. Preserve GitHub Pages compatibility unless the deployment model changes intentionally
10. Keep user-facing version labels hidden from normal users
