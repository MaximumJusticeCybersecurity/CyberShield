## 1. Core business case

Organizations are adopting AI-generated recommendations and agentic workflows faster than they can prove source quality, evidence sufficiency, bounded authority, human review, and decision accountability.

CyberShield creates the decision evidence needed before the organization acts.

## 2. One-line positioning

```text
Before relying on AI, know whether the recommendation is defensible.
```

## 3. Buyer problem

Teams are beginning to rely on AI-generated recommendations in vendor risk, cybersecurity, compliance, procurement, governance, and executive decision-making.

The risk is not only that AI can be wrong.

The larger risk is that the organization may not be able to prove:

   - what the AI recommended
   - what claims were embedded
   - what evidence was available
   - what evidence was missing
   - what assumptions remained
   - what the risk was if wrong
   - who reviewed the decision
   - whether human review was meaningful
   - why the final action was defensible

## 4. First commercial wedge

The first CyberShield wedge is vendor-risk AI Decision Assurance.

Example:

```text
AI recommends approving a vendor because the vendor has SOC 2, encrypts data, and appears low risk.
```

CyberShield shows whether that recommendation is defensible enough for enterprise action.

The expected first-demo outcome remains:

```text
Recommended Action: Request Evidence
Risk If Wrong: High
Confidence Band: Low Confidence
Human Review Required: Yes
```

## 5. First paid offer

The first paid offer should be a software-assisted advisory pilot.

Pilot scope:

   - 3 to 5 AI-generated recommendations
   - vendor-risk first
   - security-risk or compliance-control recommendations only if tied to evidence review
   - one AI Trust Decision Record per recommendation
   - short executive summary of decision risk and evidence gaps

## 6. Pilot outputs

The pilot produces:

   - AI Trust Decision Records
   - material claims extracted from the AI recommendation
   - evidence-gap summaries
   - contradictory-evidence findings
   - Risk If Wrong classification
   - Confidence Band
   - Human Review Required status
   - Meaningful Human Authority notes
   - Harness Health findings
   - strongest defensible next action
   - executive-ready decision brief

## 7. Why buyers care

CyberShield helps teams:

   - avoid acting on polished but unsupported AI output
   - save time finding weak assumptions
   - expose missing evidence faster
   - know when human review is required
   - create a defensible record before the decision is challenged
   - preserve accountability when AI influences decisions
   - reduce vendor, compliance, security, and governance surprises

Primary likely buyers and influencers:

   - CISO
   - vCISO
   - GRC leader
   - vendor-risk leader
   - procurement risk leader
   - AI governance leader
   - CIO or CTO with AI adoption responsibility
   - CAIO where the role exists

Best first audience:

```text
Teams that must defend AI-influenced vendor-risk, security, or compliance decisions.
```

## 9. Why now

AI use is moving from drafting and summarizing into recommendations, workflows, and agentic action.

As that happens, organizations need a way to prove that decisions were based on evidence and accountable review, not merely confident AI output.

The market need is shifting from:

```text
Can AI produce an answer?
```

to:

```text
Can we defend acting on that answer?
```

## 10. What CyberShield is not

CyberShield is not:

   - a finished production SaaS platform in this phase
   - a live LLM-backed analyzer unless implemented
   - a compliance certification tool
   - a legal determination tool
   - an autonomous vendor approval system
   - production CRM infrastructure
   - a generic chatbot
   - the full Aegis OS

## 11. What makes CyberShield different

CyberShield is not trying to produce another AI answer.

CyberShield produces a decision record.

It separates:

   - what AI claimed
   - what evidence supports
   - what CyberShield inferred
   - what the human decided
   - what risk remains
   - what action is most defensible

## 12. Pilot pricing posture

The first offer should be positioned as advisory plus software-assisted evidence review.

Do not price it like pure SaaS yet.

The buyer is paying for:

   - expert-led decision assurance
   - structured review of AI-generated recommendations
   - reusable decision records
   - a practical governance artifact
   - early access to the CyberShield workflow

## 13. Main sales thesis

```text
CyberShield turns AI confidence into accountable decision evidence.
```

Alternate version:

```text
CyberShield helps organizations defend AI-influenced decisions before they act.
```

## 14. Buyer proof point

The buyer should see that a recommendation can be confident, reasonable-sounding, and still not defensible.

That is the aha moment.
