# 20260605xxV17 Demo Script and Sales Walkthrough Plan

## Purpose

Turn the CyberShield Decision Assurance prototype into a demo that can be presented without improvising.

## Goal

Give the presenter a clear path from buyer pain to live workflow to executive artifact.

## Core Demo Question

```text
If your organization acted on an AI recommendation and that decision was challenged later, what evidence would you show?
```

## Demo Promise

CyberShield turns AI-assisted recommendations into defensible Trust Decision Records.

## Required Demo Flow

1. Start at package hub.
2. Open Vendor-Risk Core.
3. Explain the frozen workflow.
4. Open ATDR workbench.
5. Load Vendor-Risk: Contradictory Evidence.
6. Show the AI recommendation.
7. Show extracted claims.
8. Show synthetic evidence.
9. Show Gaps and contradictions.
10. Show Risk If Wrong and Confidence.
11. Show Review and override preservation.
12. Show Audit trail.
13. Print / Save PDF executive brief.
14. Explain pilot offer.

## Buyer Talk Tracks

### CISO / vCISO

Focus on security accountability, risk acceptance, review gates, and evidence before action.

### GRC / Compliance

Focus on evidence sufficiency, limitations, framework relevance, and avoiding unsupported compliance claims.

### Vendor-Risk / Procurement

Focus on SOC 2 scope, DPA language, subprocessors, incident notification, and approval defensibility.

### Executive / Board Advisor

Focus on accountability, hindsight, decision evidence, and what record should exist before action.

## Common Objections

### We already review AI outputs.

Response: CyberShield does not assume review is absent. It documents whether review is consistent, evidence-based, and defensible later.

### This sounds like AI judging AI.

Response: CyberShield does not ask another AI to bless the answer. It extracts claims, maps evidence, exposes missing support, requires human review, and exports the decision record.

### We have GRC tools already.

Response: CyberShield creates the decision evidence that can feed GRC. It is not replacing GRC in this prototype.

### SOC 2 should be enough.

Response: SOC 2 is evidence, not automatic approval. Scope, date, service coverage, data use, subprocessors, and contract terms still matter.
