# 20260605xxV1 ATDR Pilot Offer

## Offer Name

```text
CyberShield AI Recommendation Assurance Sprint
```

## Plain-English Description

CyberShield helps your organization review AI-generated security, compliance, and vendor-risk recommendations before teams act on them.

The sprint converts selected AI-generated recommendations into AI Trust Decision Records that document claims, evidence, missing support, Risk If Wrong, confidence, human review requirements, and defensible next actions.

## Who It Is For

- CISO
- vCISO
- GRC leader
- Compliance owner
- Vendor-risk owner
- AI governance leader
- Security architecture leader
- Procurement leader for AI vendors
- Executive sponsor managing AI risk

## Problem It Solves

Organizations are already using AI-generated recommendations to influence real decisions.

The risk is that a confident AI recommendation may hide:

- Unsupported claims
- Missing evidence
- Weak evidence
- Stale evidence
- Scope mismatch
- Compliance overclaims
- Vendor self-attestation
- Human review gaps
- Risk acceptance without an accountable owner

## Sprint Scope

Recommended entry scope:

```text
3 to 5 AI-generated recommendations
2 to 4 weeks
1 executive readout
```

Potential recommendation types:

- AI vendor approval recommendation
- Vendor-risk approval recommendation
- Vulnerability risk acceptance recommendation
- Compliance control mapping recommendation
- Security control implementation recommendation
- Policy adequacy recommendation
- Customer-facing AI assurance language recommendation

## Deliverables

- AI Trust Decision Record for each reviewed recommendation
- Claim extraction table
- Evidence map
- Missing evidence and gap summary
- Risk If Wrong classification
- Confidence band and rationale
- Human review requirement
- Recommended action
- Executive findings brief
- Recommended workflow controls

## Pilot Workflow

### Step 1: Intake

Client provides AI-generated recommendations and available evidence.

### Step 2: ATDR Analysis

CyberShield extracts claims, maps evidence, identifies gaps, classifies Risk If Wrong, assigns confidence, and identifies review gates.

### Step 3: Human Review

Dr. Max Justice, vCISO | Security SME | Cybersecurity SME, reviews findings and validates the decision posture.

### Step 4: Executive Readout

Client receives AI Trust Decision Records and an executive summary of patterns, risks, and recommended controls.

## Example Sprint Outcomes

- AI recommendation was not defensible because material evidence was missing
- Vendor approval claim relied too heavily on SOC 2 without scope review
- Compliance claim treated policy existence as implementation evidence
- Security risk acceptance lacked asset exposure and risk owner approval
- Human review was required before action

## Best First Close

Ask:

```text
Can we identify three AI-generated recommendations your organization may act on in the next 30 days and turn them into defensible decision records?
```

## Success Criteria

A pilot succeeds when the client can point to specific AI-generated recommendations and say:

```text
We now know what claims were made, what evidence supports them, what is missing, what happens if the AI is wrong, who must review it, and what action is defensible.
```
