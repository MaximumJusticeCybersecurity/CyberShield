# 20260605xxV9 ATDR Discovery Call Script

## Opening

Thank you for taking a look at CyberShield AI Decision Assurance.

I am not looking for polite feedback.  I am trying to learn where this prevents a real bad AI-influenced decision.

The workflow is simple:

```text
AI-generated recommendation in -> AI Trust Decision Record out
```

## Frame

The question is not whether the AI answer sounds right.

The question is:

```text
Can the organization defend acting on the recommendation based on the evidence available at the time?
```

## Core Questions

1. Where are you already seeing people act on AI-generated recommendations before the recommendation is properly reviewed?
2. Which AI-generated recommendation type is most dangerous if wrong?
3. Who inside an organization would feel this pain first?
4. Who would have budget to solve it?
5. What would they compare this against?
6. Does “AI Trust Decision Record” feel like the right artifact name?
7. Does “CyberShield creates decision evidence” land?
8. Would a 3 to 5 recommendation pilot be easy to understand and buy?
9. What would make the pilot more valuable?
10. What would make you dismiss this as AI judging AI?

## Strong Closing Question

```text
If your organization made a bad AI-influenced decision six months from now, what record would you wish existed before the decision was made?
```

## Must-Ask Question

```text
Where would this prevent a bad AI-influenced decision?
```

## Listen For

Strong signal:

- Specific use case
- Specific title
- Budget owner
- Urgent risk
- Pilot interest
- Request for executive brief

Weak signal:

- “Interesting” with no use case
- No buyer named
- No decision owner
- Treated as a dashboard feature
- No urgency

## Close

If the pain is real, ask:

```text
Could we identify three AI-generated recommendations that may influence action soon and turn them into AI Trust Decision Records?
```
