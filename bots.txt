Core thesis:
CyberShield evaluates whether the information behind a critical action can be trusted before the business acts.

Core question:
Can we trust the information behind this action before the business acts?

Trust Model doctrine:
Executives rarely have perfect information. CyberShield is not about perfect evidence. Evidence supports the Trust Model, but evidence volume is not the point. Information reliability, source confidence, owner accountability, verification path, and consequence if wrong are the point.

TrustMap doctrine:
The CyberShield Core is the TrustMap kernel, not a normal node. TrustMap must show one enterprise trust universe: Kernel is CyberShield Core; Layer 1 is major trust domains; Layer 2 is domain assets, systems, vendors, data classes, controls, workflows, and entities; Layer 3 is small tagged risk/evidence/action/owner/consequence dots. Scenario views must highlight paths without replacing the universe.
