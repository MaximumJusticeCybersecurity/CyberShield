Public naming rule:
Public product name is CyberShield Executive OS. Current prototype build is V60.3. Do not call the public build CyberShield OS v8 unless the repo, README, loader, Settings/admin metadata, and public UX are intentionally changed to that version scheme.

Controlling scoring doctrine:
CyberShield must not show decorative scores. Every score must trace to a named, versioned model with factors, weights, evidence inputs, confidence logic, missing evidence, assumptions, risk if wrong, and action consequence.

Guiding principle:
No score without a model. No model without evidence. No evidence without traceability. No action without consequence.

Model maturity rule:
CyberShield scoring models are Expert-Derived V1 Trust Scoring Models. They are not Statistically Validated Trust Models. The architecture has high confidence because it separates trust, confidence, evidence quality, reliance risk, runtime admissibility, and defensibility. The initial factors have medium confidence. The default weights have lower confidence until calibrated against test scenarios, expert review, red-team attempts, and real-world use cases.
