TrustMap visual doctrine:
The radar and constellation background is the environment. The Layer 1 graphical trust assets are the interface. The active decision is the gravity center.

Baseline Layer 1 TrustMap assets:
- Cloud & Infrastructure
- Identities & Access
- Applications & Data
- AI Systems & Agents
- Third Parties & Vendors
- Devices & Endpoints
- CMMC & Compliance

Score rule:
Every visible score must be clickable and must explain or route to the model, evidence, assumptions, missing inputs, confidence, reliance risk, and decision rationale behind that score.

Trust distribution rule:
Operational Trust Score is a current decision posture score. Trust Level Distribution is a count or percentage distribution across Layer 1 trust assets. They should not show the same number unless intentionally representing the same metric.

Controlling scoring doctrine:
CyberShield must not show decorative scores. Every score must trace to a named, versioned model with factors, weights, evidence inputs, confidence logic, missing evidence, assumptions, risk if wrong, and action consequence.

Guiding principle:
No score without a model. No model without evidence. No evidence without traceability. No action without consequence.
