Core thesis:
CyberShield evaluates whether a consequential claim or action should be trusted before it is acted upon.

Core question:
Can we trust the information behind this action or claim before the business acts, cites, shares, briefs, teaches, or relies on it?

Trust Model doctrine:
Executives rarely have perfect information. CyberShield is not about perfect evidence. Evidence supports the Trust Model, but evidence volume is not the point. Information reliability, source confidence, owner accountability, verification path, consequence if wrong, and decision reliance are the point.

TrustMap visual doctrine:
The CyberShield TrustMap is a radar-constellation interface. Radar means orientation, active scanning, and situational awareness. Constellation means connected assets, dependencies, and trust relationships. It must not look like a process-flow diagram.

V55.1 TrustMap rules:
- CyberShield Core is a beveled shield boundary and trust kernel, not a normal node
- shield interior must stay clean: MJC logo, CyberShield Core, organization name, Trust Kernel
- connector lines must not visibly pass through or clutter the shield interior
- Layer 1 domains are neon-blue graphical trust objects, not cards
- Fit Map is for orientation, not reading
- Domain View is for interpretation
- Object View is for detail
- Proof Pack is for documentation
- connectors are straight point-to-point lines
- connector thickness decreases by depth
- core-to-domain connectors are thickest and neon blue
- domain-to-asset connectors are medium
- asset-to-tag connectors are thin and subtle
- cross-domain links stay faint until selected or scenario-highlighted

Purpose Protocol doctrine:
CyberShield turns purpose into protocol. Purpose is not governance until it can cause a refusal. Purpose Protocol converts executive intent, mission, risk appetite, and operating principles into machine-readable constraints, trade-off rules, refusal conditions, evidence requirements, escalation paths, and visible human ownership before action.

Priority Purpose Protocol scenario:
Vendor payment destination change. If banking details changed within 30 days, payment approval is refused unless current banking verification and controller approval are present.
