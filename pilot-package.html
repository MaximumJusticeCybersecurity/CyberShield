CyberShield Controlled AI Decision Assurance Pilot

Review 3 to 5 AI-generated recommendations in one defined decision domain. The initial proof point is vendor risk, with security or compliance recommendations included only when they are tied to an evidence-based decision review.

Record-firstExpert-assistedNot production SaaS

See the Vendor-Risk ExampleDiscuss a Controlled Pilot

Input

3 to 5 AI-generated recommendations your organization is considering, plus the available evidence and decision context.

Process

CyberShield separates material claims, maps evidence, identifies gaps and contradictions, classifies Risk If Wrong, applies defined checks, and preserves required human review.

Output

An AI Trust Decision Record for each recommendation, plus an executive findings briefing and review-process improvements.

  • Risk If Wrong and confidence assessment.

  • Participate in a scope and kickoff conversation.
  • Transfer the approved recommendations and available evidence through an agreed method.
  • Answer targeted context questions.
  • Participate in the findings review.

What the Pilot Proves

The pilot succeeds when the organization can see where an AI-generated recommendation sounds reasonable but exceeds the evidence, what additional validation would matter, who must review the decision, and what action the current evidence can defensibly support.

Human authority remains superior

CyberShield does not automatically approve a vendor, accept risk, certify compliance, or replace the accountable decision owner. It makes the recommendation, evidence, limitations, consequence, and review path more defensible.

Explicit Limitations

  • This is not positioned as a finished multi-tenant SaaS platform.
  • This is not a live autonomous approval system.
  • This is not compliance certification.
  • This is not production CRM infrastructure.
  • Framework mappings are relevance mappings, not proof of compliance.
  • SOC 2 does not equal automatic vendor approval.
  • Results depend on the completeness, relevance, freshness, scope, and independence of the evidence supplied.
