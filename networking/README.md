Version timestamp: 2026070914

## Purpose

Preserve relationship-specific context, meeting preparation, questions, commitments, follow-up, and learning for people who materially influence CyberShield, Aegis, Maximum Justice Cybersecurity, and related missions.

This directory is not a conventional contact list.  It is durable relationship intelligence that helps Dr. Max Justice and his team prepare thoughtfully, remember prior conversations, honor commitments, and improve future engagement.

## Directory structure

Create one subdirectory per person or relationship using the person's commonly used name:

```text
networking/
  Sandeep/
  Another-Person/
```

Each relationship directory should grow through dated records rather than one endlessly overwritten document.

Recommended contents:

```text
README.md
YYYYMMDDHH-meeting-agenda.md
YYYYMMDDHH-prioritized-questions.md
YYYYMMDDHH-meeting-notes.md
YYYYMMDDHH-follow-up.md
YYYYMMDDHH-relationship-update.md
```

## Required relationship record fields

Where known and appropriate, preserve:

- person and preferred name;
- role and organization;
- relationship history;
- why the relationship matters;
- prior discussions and commitments;
- current interests and likely concerns;
- meeting objective;
- questions to ask;
- questions they asked;
- advice or feedback received;
- commitments made by either party;
- follow-up owner and due date;
- source or evidence for important facts; and
- responsible author of each record.

## Operating rules

1. Preserve facts, observations, interpretations, and assumptions as separate categories.
2. Do not infer personal beliefs, confidential information, or organizational authority without evidence.
3. Record what was actually said rather than polishing history after the fact.
4. Distinguish relationship insight from product requirements.
5. Promote durable product or architecture decisions into the proper requirements or governance directory instead of leaving them only in networking notes.
6. Keep agendas realistic for the time available.
7. Prioritize a few high-value questions over a long list that cannot be asked.
8. Record commitments immediately after meetings.
9. Avoid storing credentials, private authentication data, or unnecessary sensitive personal information.
10. Identify the responsible author of every material update.

## Relationship principle

The objective is not to extract value from people.  It is to build informed, reciprocal, trusted relationships through preparation, attention, follow-through, and respect for their time.
