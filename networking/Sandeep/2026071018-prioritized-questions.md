## Question strategy

Do not treat this as a ten-question interview.

Ask **two questions at most**.  Use the backup only if one primary question was already answered during the demonstration or becomes irrelevant.

Allow Sandeep to answer fully.  A deep answer to one question is more valuable than shallow answers to several.

## Primary Question 1: Differentiation and weakest assumption

> From your perspective, what is the weakest assumption in our thesis that CyberShield is meaningfully different from a second-model review or an AI governance checklist?

### Why this question earns the time

It invites Sandeep to attack the central product thesis rather than comment on superficial features.

Listen for:

- whether the Trust Decision Record is a meaningful differentiator;
- whether evidence and provenance are sufficiently independent from the analyzing model;
- whether the deterministic layer is credible;
- whether the workflow solves an enterprise problem rather than a founder-defined problem;
- whether another existing product already occupies the position; and
- what would need to change for the differentiation to become defensible.

### One follow-up only

> What would you change first to strengthen that distinction?

## Primary Question 2: Smallest credible paid pilot

> What is the smallest enterprise pilot you believe someone would pay to test, and what evidence would it need to produce for the pilot to be considered credible?

### Why this question earns the time

It forces the discussion from abstract architecture into buyer, scope, outcome, and proof.

Listen for:

- best first decision domain;
- likely buyer or accountable owner;
- number and type of recommendations;
- required inputs and evidence;
- acceptable manual versus automated work;
- success and failure criteria;
- defensibility expectations;
- integration expectations;
- pilot duration and boundaries; and
- who could authorize or sponsor such a pilot.

### One follow-up only

> Who inside an enterprise would feel this problem strongly enough to sponsor that pilot?

## Backup Question: Deterministic versus model-assisted judgment

Use only when one primary question was substantially answered during the demo.

> In this workflow, where should deterministic rules stop and model-assisted judgment begin?

Listen for:

- schema and completeness rules;
- claim extraction;
- evidence relevance;
- risk classification;
- confidence ceilings;
- candidate-action eligibility;
- review routing;
- rationale generation;
- human judgment; and
- unacceptable model discretion.
