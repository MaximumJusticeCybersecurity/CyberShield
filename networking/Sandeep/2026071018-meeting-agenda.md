### 2:00-4:00 | Frame the problem and product thesis

Core problem:

> AI recommendations often arrive polished, confident, and ready for action.  The missing question is whether the organization can defend acting on them.

Product contract:

```text
AI Recommendation In
Trust Decision Record Out
```

Positioning:

> CyberShield helps an accountable human determine whether an AI-generated recommendation is defensible enough to act upon.

### 4:00-21:00 | Demonstrate the current vendor-risk workflow

This is the majority of the meeting.

#### A. Recommendation under review

Use the controlled recommendation:

> AI recommends approving Vendor X because they have a SOC 2 report, encrypt customer data, and appear low risk.

Ask Sandeep for his initial instinct before revealing CyberShield's result.

#### B. Extracted material claims

Show that the recommendation contains several distinct claims, not one conclusion:

- the vendor should be approved;
- the SOC 2 report exists and is current;
- the report covers the relevant service;
- encryption is implemented and supported;
- customer-data use is acceptable;
- subprocessors are understood;
- incident notification is adequate;
- the vendor is low risk; and
- SOC 2 plus encryption is sufficient for approval.

Emphasize the unsupported leap from partial controls to approval.

#### C. Evidence, gaps, assumptions, and contradictions

Show:

- what evidence supports each claim;
- where evidence is self-attested;
- where scope is unclear;
- what evidence is missing;
- what assumptions the recommendation depends upon;
- what evidence contradicts or qualifies the recommendation; and
- why business comfort is not accountable approval.

#### D. Separate the five decision concepts

Show these independently:

1. Evidence Sufficiency
2. Confidence
3. Risk If Wrong
4. Strongest Defensible Action
5. Human Review Requirement

Use this explanation:

> The weak evidence lowered confidence.  The high consequence did not lower confidence.  It raised the evidence threshold and the level of oversight required before action.

#### E. Decision and record

Show the current controlled result:

- Evidence Sufficiency: Insufficient
- Confidence: Low
- Risk If Wrong: High
- Strongest Defensible Action: Request Evidence
- Human Review: Vendor-Risk Owner, Security SME, Legal or Privacy Reviewer, and Business Owner

Then show the Trust Decision Record preserving:

- original recommendation;
- claims;
- evidence and provenance;
- missing evidence;
- assumptions and contradictions;
- consequence;
- confidence explanation;
- candidate actions;
- selected action;
- required review;
- human decision and override; and
- residual risk.
