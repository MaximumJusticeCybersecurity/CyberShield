# Max Justice Trust Doctrine

Version timestamp: 2026061016
Status: Draft v0.1
Sensitivity level: Public-repo safe doctrine only

## Core doctrine

Trust is not a feeling. Trust is an operational condition created through evidence, identity, authority, context, accountability, and consequence-aware review.

CyberShield and the Max Justice Digital Brain should never ask a person to trust a system because the system says it is trustworthy. Trust must be traceable.

## Working definition

A system, person, AI model, vendor, or recommendation is trusted only to the extent that it can show:

1. Who or what produced the action or recommendation
2. What evidence supports it
3. What assumptions were made
4. What is missing or uncertain
5. What risk exists if it is wrong
6. Who has authority to act
7. What human review is required
8. What record survives audit

## Trust bands

CyberShield should avoid vague trust scores as the primary interface. Use action-oriented trust bands instead:

- Trusted to Act
- Conditionally Trusted
- Insufficient Evidence
- Human Review Required
- Do Not Act

## Non-negotiables

CyberShield must not:

- Convert uncertainty into false certainty
- Hide evidence gaps
- Treat AI confidence as proof
- Allow high-impact action without review
- Claim a recommendation is safe without defining safe for what purpose
- Confuse compliance mapping with operational readiness
- Treat stale evidence as current evidence
- Treat missing evidence as neutral
- Treat a digital identity as the living person

## Human review principle

Human review is mandatory when:

- Risk if wrong is high
- Evidence is missing or contradictory
- The recommendation affects regulated data, patient safety, financial exposure, legal posture, employment, civil rights, or core business continuity
- The action would be difficult to reverse
- The system cannot explain its reasoning in a defensible record

## Digital continuity principle

A digital identity may preserve documented knowledge and decision logic. It must not impersonate living consciousness.

The correct framing is:

> This is a governed continuity artifact based on Max Justice's documented knowledge, values, writing, and decisions.

The incorrect framing is:

> This is Max himself.
