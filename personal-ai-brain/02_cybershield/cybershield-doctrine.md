# CyberShield Doctrine

Version timestamp: 2026061016
Status: Draft v0.1
Sensitivity level: Public-repo safe doctrine only

## Current wedge

The first practical CyberShield workflow is:

> AI-generated recommendation in. AI Trust Decision Record out.

The first version should focus on security, compliance, and vendor-risk recommendations.

## Primary question

CyberShield v0.1 should answer one question clearly:

> Can this AI-generated recommendation be trusted enough to act on?

It should not try to answer every trust question in the enterprise at once.

## Required workflow

CyberShield should:

1. Receive an AI-generated recommendation
2. Extract discrete claims
3. Map claims to evidence
4. Identify missing support
5. Identify contradictions
6. Classify risk if wrong
7. Assign an action-oriented confidence band
8. Recommend action
9. Require human review where needed
10. Export a defensible decision record

## First demo target

Recommended first demo target:

> AI-generated vendor-risk recommendation

Reason: vendor risk is evidence-heavy, budget-connected, common, and easier for buyers to understand than broad AI governance.

## Product boundaries

Do not prioritize these before the record workflow works:

- Broad dashboards
- Generic trust scores
- Full TrustMap
- Runtime agents
- Autonomous execution
- Broad governance portals
- Abstract AI ethics modules

## Bigger vision

The broader CyberShield vision is a trusted-entity map for the digital realm. It should eventually represent people, vendors, AI systems, agents, policies, evidence, credentials, assets, approvals, models, and decision records.

Long-term CyberShield should evolve from:

> Can we trust AI?

To:

> Can we trust digitally mediated human intent?

## Aegis role

Aegis is the CyberShield-aligned protective role. It represents the trust-guide function: evaluate evidence, map authority, identify uncertainty, preserve decision records, and prevent blind trust in AI or digital systems.
